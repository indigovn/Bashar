## This AND That vs. This OR That — The Core Principle

### The Two Perspectives

| Perspective | Mechanism | Orientation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **This AND That** | Inclusivity — everything is valid, all perspectives coexist | Higher Mind's perspective |
| **This OR That** | Exclusivity — choosing one thing, excluding others | Physical Mind's perspective |

### How They Work Together

> "By having the this and that perspective, it will allow you to see the bigger picture that includes all of those positions, all of those descriptions, all of those definitions that work for you."

> "The this or that scenario starts excluding other definitions as being impossible, improbable, not capable of actually occurring."

**Both are needed**:

```
THIS AND THAT (Higher Mind / Inclusivity)
  = Everything is valid, all possibilities exist
  = The expanded view
  = Mechanically geared toward the POSITIVE
              ↕
THIS OR THAT (Physical Mind / Exclusivity)
  = Choose one thing from the infinite
  = The focused experience
  = Mechanically geared toward the NEGATIVE
  = Gives you the concept of CHOICE
              ↕
TOGETHER they create physical experience
  = You need BOTH to have the kinds
    of experiences you are having
```

### The Roles of Each Mind

> "The physical mind is the excluder. The higher mind is the includer. And you need to allow them to work together in harmony and balance."

| Physical Mind | Higher Mind |
|--------------|-------------|
| Focuses, excludes, selects | Includes everything |
| Creates the experience of choice | Knows everything is valid |
| "This OR that" | "This AND that" |
| Does not know HOW things will happen | Knows HOW — let it do its job |
| Is a focus tool | Is the expanded awareness |

> "Everything to the higher mind is always this and that. Even though more often than not to the physical mind it seems only to be this or that."

### The Balance Point

> "Do not get caught up in those conundrums. Do not get caught up in those mazes of confusion that it's got to be this or it's got to be that or it doesn't make sense. It can make perfect sense being this and that if you simply find the balance point between the two."

> "They are not really in opposition even though they may seem to be opposites. Both have to come from the same source. Both have to come from the same center."

## The Three Primary Colors of Light

### Light vs. Pigment

| Type | Primary Colors |
|------|---------------|
| **Light** (what Bashar uses) | Red, Blue, Green |
| **Pigment** (paint/ink) | Red, Blue, Yellow |

### How They Combine

```
Primary Colors of LIGHT:

        RED ●
       / \
      /   \
VIOLET ●───● ORANGE
    /   ●   \
   /  WHITE  \
  /   LIGHT   \
BLUE ●───────● GREEN
       YELLOW

RED + BLUE = VIOLET
RED + GREEN = ORANGE
BLUE + GREEN = YELLOW
RED + BLUE + GREEN = WHITE LIGHT
```

### The Vesica Piscis

When the three circles of primary color overlap, the intersections create **vesica piscis** shapes containing the secondary colors (violet, yellow, orange). Where all three overlap in the center: **pure white light**.

### Infinite Shades

> "In each primary color there are nearly an infinite number of shades of red, an infinite number of shades of blue, an infinite number of shades of green."

These different shades result from the secondary vibrations mixing in different ratios with the primaries.
