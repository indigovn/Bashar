## The Power Test — Inside vs. Outside

### How to Discern

> "The way to understand the difference is to simply observe what type of energy is being utilized in the creation of that scenario. If the individuals are placing the power outside of themselves, then it is an act in a negative direction. If they understand that the power is within them and that they are a part of infinite creation, then you will not see them in such great expression of need."

### The Direct Test

> "The way for an individual to discern for himself or herself whether the excitement they feel is genuine excitement or anxiety is to examine whether they are moving toward whatever it is that seems to be producing the excitement with a sense that the power lies within them — or whether they're reaching out thinking that that's going to solve everything for them."

| Direction of Power | Type of Energy |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Power INSIDE you, you move WITH inner certainty | True excitement |
| Power OUTSIDE you, you REACH OUT for a fix | Anxiety in disguise |
| "I am part of infinite creation" | True excitement |
| "I need this tool/substance/person to solve me" | Anxiety in disguise |

## Pretending Anxiety Is Excitement to Avoid Facing Things

### The Hidden Mechanism

> "It is a discounting of many portions and an invalidation of many portions of themselves. They think it is excitement to overlook the fact of the things they fear to face about themselves because they think that it will be an ordeal to face those things and they do not want to face that. So they will pretend that the anxiety they feel is truly excitement when it isn't."

This is one of Bashar's most penetrating insights: addiction is anxiety pretending to be excitement, used to avoid facing what we fear within ourselves.

## The Discernment Method

### Practical Application

Whenever you feel strong "excitement" toward something:

1. **Where is the power?** Inside you or outside you?
2. **What's the relationship?** Are you bringing yourself TO it, or grabbing it to fix yourself?
3. **What's the underlying belief?** "I am whole and curious" or "I need this to be okay"?
4. **What are you avoiding?** Is this drawing you toward something — or away from yourself?

If power is outside, if you're grabbing to fix yourself, if you're avoiding facing yourself — that's anxiety wearing excitement's clothes.
