# Bashar: The Journey of Stepping Stones, Two Kinds of Synchronicity, Archetypes & You Are a Path - Complete Teaching

## Overview

This transmission opens with one of Bashar's most powerful reframes of the spiritual journey: there is no ultimate destination. Every moment is a stepping stone, every experience a step along the way — but not to somewhere else. The "way" is always here. It moves through essential teachings: the only true goal is **being yourself**, every stepping stone must be savored fully or you create gaps you cannot cross, there is no clock and nothing runs out of time for what's relevant, synchronicity has **two forms** (the "gas pedal" bringing opportunities AND the "brake" preventing inappropriate actions), how to distinguish true love from insistence in relationships (sometimes the most loving thing is to leave), the archetypal level as the collective unconscious of the entire planet (including every species), the origin of the Sassanian Empire name (traced back to a Sassani entity mistaken for a god), the distinction between **inspiration** (imagination-higher mind connection) and **motivation** (belief-system willingness to act), how invalidating what you don't prefer removes stepping stones and creates gaps, and climaxes with the mind-altering teaching: **you are not on a path — you ARE a path**. Every stepping stone is the same place, reconfigured. You don't move through space-time; **space-time moves through you**.

## Two Kinds of Synchronicity — The Gas Pedal AND the Brake

### The First Kind

> "The first kind most of you are familiar with — that's the one you normally talk about. The synchronicity of something being brought to you. That's an opportunity to act on."

### The Second Kind

> "The other kind of synchronicity is the kind of synchronicity that actually **prevents you** from acting on things that are not appropriate for you to act on at that particular moment."

### The Driving Analogy

> "Synchronicity is not only (to use a simplistic analogy) is not only the gas pedal saying 'go ahead.' It's also the brake saying 'no, you can't go this way right now. You need to stay where you are, or you need to look for some other avenue.'"

### Why Both Are Guiding You

> "The balance between the idea of the synchronicity that says take this action and the synchronicity that says do not act in this direction **is your guiding principle in life**."

```
SYNCHRONICITY: TWO KINDS

  TYPE 1 — GAS PEDAL (Opportunity)
  ────────────────────────────────
  • Brings things into your life
  • Presents actions to take
  • Says: "Go ahead, this way"
  • Recognized as "positive synchronicity"

  TYPE 2 — BRAKE (Prevention)
  ────────────────────────────
  • Blocks inappropriate actions
  • Keeps you from moving in wrong direction
  • Says: "Not now. Stay. Try another way."
  • Often NOT recognized as synchronicity
  • Feels like "things aren't working"
  • Actually: guidance in the form of blocking
```

### Synchronicity as Organizing Principle

> "That's why synchronicity is also an organizing principle because it's really keeping you on the straight and narrow. No. No. Yes. Yes. No. No. Yes. Yes."

## Invalidation Removes the Stepping Stone from Your Path

### The Definition of Invalidation

> "Invalidation is discounting the value — discounting what's happening in your life as if it didn't belong. And therefore all you're doing is reinforcing your inability to use it in a way that positively serves you."

### The Stepping Stone Analogy

> "It's like saying: 'I see a series of stepping stones ahead of me, but one of them is a color I don't like. I don't prefer it. So I'm going to invalidate it. I'm going to say that stone has absolutely no value whatsoever. It does not belong on the path. So get rid of it.'"

> "Now you have no way to step from that stone to the next one. You have literally caused yourself to invalidate your path, to remove the ability to step forward."

### The Alternative

> "Use it to your advantage saying: 'Well, that stone is different because there must be something different I need to look at here. There must be something different I need to learn here that would actually help me then go on to the next stone.'"

```
INVALIDATION'S EFFECT ON YOUR PATH

  Path with stepping stones:
  [Stone A] → [Stone B] → [Stone C] → [Stone D]

  You dislike Stone B's "color"
              ↓
  You INVALIDATE it:
  "This doesn't belong. It has no value."
              ↓
  [Stone A] →     GAP     → [Stone C] → [Stone D]
              ↓
  You cannot cross the gap
  You are stuck at Stone A
              ↓
  THE ALTERNATIVE:
  [Stone A] → [Stone B (different)] → [Stone C] → [Stone D]
  "This stone is different — what can
   I learn here that serves me?"
              ↓
  Path intact. You continue moving.
```
