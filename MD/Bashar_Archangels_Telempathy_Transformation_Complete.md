## Telempathy — True Telepathy Is Love

### The Reframing: From Telepathy to Telempathy

Bashar introduces his specific term — **telempathy** — combining "tele" (at a distance) with "empathy" (feeling with).

> "The first fundamental element of telempathy is unconditional love."

### What Telempathy Is NOT

> "It's not a mind thing."

You are **never** reading anyone's mind. That is a fundamental misunderstanding of how telepathic communication works.

### What Telempathy IS

> "It's being on the same wavelength as something else."

> "You are simply having the same thoughts at the same time because you're on the same wavelength because you are in love with them."

### The Mechanism

```
Unconditional Love
        ↓
Same Wavelength / Same Frequency
        ↓
Same Thoughts Arise Simultaneously
        ↓
Synchronicity — No "First" or "Sender/Receiver"
        ↓
Telempathy
```

### Connection to Synchronicity

The questioner observes that this is what synchronicities are — it's hard to tell who had the experience first, or whether there even was a "first."

> Bashar confirms: That IS telempathy.

### Key Principles

- Telempathy is **activated by unconditional love**, not mental effort
- It is **not** mind-reading — it's frequency-matching
- When you are in love with something, you are on the same wavelength
- Being on the same wavelength means you **have the same thoughts at the same time**
- There is no sender and receiver — it is simultaneous arising
- This is the mechanism behind synchronicity itself

## Emotions Are Gateways, Not Things to Release

### The Common Misconception

Many people try to "release emotions" — especially suppressed ones from childhood. A questioner shares her experience with Ayahuasca opening a portal to suppressed emotions.

### Bashar's Correction

> "It's not so much about releasing emotions as it is releasing the definitions that don't work for you that create the emotions."

> "Emotions don't exist in a vacuum. You can't have an emotion or a feeling about anything if you don't have a definition first."

### Own the Emotions, Release the Definitions

> "The emotions have to be owned, not released. The emotions have to be delved deeper into to find the definitions that are generating them. Then when you release the definitions, the emotions that go with them in a sense will simply evaporate."

### The Process

```
Feel the Emotion Fully (OWN it)
        ↓
Delve DEEPER Into It
        ↓
Discover the DEFINITION Generating It
        ↓
Release the DEFINITION (not the emotion)
        ↓
The Emotion Evaporates Naturally
        ↓
New Understanding / New Choice Emerges
```

### The Questioner's Technique

The questioner confirms this matches her experience:

> "I've noticed that once I feel the emotion through, the answer comes in at the end. It presents itself — how I should have been acting... So I wouldn't create this."

Bashar validates this as her personal **permission slip** — her technique for using emotions to discover definitions and let go of them.

### Key Principles

| Approach | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Try to release emotions directly | They persist or return |
| Own emotions and delve deeper | Discover the underlying definition |
| Release the definition | Emotions evaporate naturally |
| New understanding emerges | Freedom to choose differently |

## The Mechanics of Fear — Why You Hold Onto What You Don't Prefer

### The Setup

A questioner from El Salvador living in the United States shares that despite following his passion, fears of losing everything keep recurring — fear of deportation, losing family, starting over.

### The Core Question

> "Why are you buying into that scenario as being more real than what you prefer?"

### Fear Is a Result, Not a Cause

> "The fear does not justify it. The fear is the result of what you're buying into as true."

> "You can't justify it by saying, 'I'm afraid.' That's the result. The cause is that you're choosing to buy into a definitional belief system that isn't what you prefer."

### The Hidden Payoff

> "What do you get out of buying into the fear scenario?"

The questioner answers "Nothing." Bashar disagrees:

> "Yes, you do. Or you wouldn't do it. None of you do anything and buy into anything that you haven't been taught to believe serves you. Even if it doesn't serve you, you wouldn't buy into it if you weren't taught to believe that it does."

### The Core Mechanism

```
You were TAUGHT to believe the negative scenario serves you
        ↓
This belief attaches to your MOTIVATIONAL MECHANISM
        ↓
You unconsciously believe the negative is MORE PROBABLE
        ↓
You keep choosing it as MORE REAL than what you prefer
        ↓
Fear is generated (the RESULT, not the cause)
        ↓
Fear reinforces the belief that the negative is more real
        ↓
Cycle continues
```

### The Key Diagnostic Questions

1. **"How is it that you believe that something you don't prefer serves you?"**
2. **"Why do you have a definition that says that the scenario you don't prefer is actually more believable, more real, more likely, more probable than the reality that you do prefer?"**
3. **"What beliefs have you attached to your motivational mechanism that makes you continue to choose to buy into a reality you say you don't prefer?"**

### The Process to Freedom

> "Every time one of those little negative ideas pops up and you look at the scenario you would prefer, it's your opportunity to see where your definitions are, where you're putting the weight of reality, which one you believe is really more probable and why."

```
Negative scenario pops into imagination
        ↓
COMPARE it to the scenario you prefer
        ↓
Ask: "What is it about the negative idea
      that I am making appear more real
      than the idea that I do prefer?"
        ↓
Examine WHERE you're putting the
"weight of reality"
        ↓
Ask WHY you believe the negative
is more probable
        ↓
Stay with this process
        ↓
FREEDOM
```

### The Promise

> "If you stay in the positive state and use it this way as a positive tool, whatever happens will be exactly what needs to happen. And it's going to benefit you and everyone that is connected to you in your life in the way that works best for all concerned."
