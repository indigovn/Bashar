# Bashar: Permission Slips, The Higher Self Mechanics & Circumstances Don't Matter - Complete Teaching

## Overview

This transmission, titled "Permission Slips," is one of the most structurally important teachings in the entire body of work. It opens by explaining that every tool, ritual, crystal, book, meditation, and technique — including channeling itself — is nothing more than a permission slip you give yourself to be who you already are. The power is never in the object; you are generating the effect from your own energy. It then delivers a revolutionary breakdown of consciousness into three levels: the Higher Self conceives, the physical brain receives, the physical mind perceives — and the physical mind NEVER conceives ideas (this is freeing, not limiting: you can stop trying to do your higher self's job). It explains why the physical mind can only perceive how something HAPPENED but never how something HAPPENS, why trying to figure it out is exhausting because you weren't hired for that job, redefines "circumstances don't matter / only state of being matters" with the wordplay that circumstances don't MATTER-ialize things while state of being MATTER-ializes things, demonstrates the belief investigation process live with a questioner, addresses the self-reinforcing nature of beliefs, and defines enlightenment as simply "the willingness to lighten up."

## How Something Happened vs. How Something Happens

### The Critical Distinction

> "Physical mind cannot perceive how something happens. It can only perceive how something happened."

### Why This Matters

> "Because the physical mind thinks it's coming up with these things, it then takes what happened and assumes that that's how things happen. And it's not. It's only what happened that time."

### The Trap

> "It thinks, 'Well, if I do that again, I can make this happen.' No, you might get some degree of repetitional success with that philosophy, but it will never go beyond the parameters of what you observed in that particular happenstance."

> "What happened was designed to allow that particular thing to happen. And that particular thing doesn't necessarily require the same ingredients as something else."

### The Repetition Trap

> "When you may want something else to happen, when you may want to expand on what happened, you cannot use the same ingredients that made that happen because then all you do is repeat the cycle and wonder why you're not getting anywhere."

```
THE PHYSICAL MIND'S LIMITATION

  Something HAPPENED
      ↓
  Physical mind observes HOW it happened
      ↓
  Physical mind assumes: "That's how
  things happen in general"
      ↓
  Physical mind tries to repeat
  the same ingredients
      ↓
  Gets stuck in repetition
      ↓
  Wonders: "Why am I not getting anywhere?"

  THE TRUTH:
  What happened was designed for THAT
  specific thing only.
  Different things require DIFFERENT ingredients.
  Only the higher self knows what those are.
```

### Not Faith — Understanding

> "This is why you talk about such things as, 'Well, you just have to take it on faith.' No, you don't. It's really about knowing and understanding the nature of your actual existence."

| What You're Doing | What You Should Be Doing |
|------------------|------------------------|
| Trying to figure out the HOW | Trusting the higher self to show you |
| Seeing the entire road from start to finish | Seeing just a few hundred feet ahead |
| Doing the higher self's job | Doing YOUR job: perceiving and choosing meaning |

```
THE WORDPLAY OF REALITY

  "Only state of being MATTERS"
```

## All Emotions Come from Beliefs First — The Investigation Process

### The Law

> "All emotions without exception, all emotions, all feelings only exist because there is a belief that exists first. You cannot have a feeling without having a definition or a belief first."

### The Investigation Process

> "If you have a bad feeling, acknowledge it. 'All right, I've got a bad feeling. So what?' Now I ask myself, 'Do I prefer this feeling?' 'Well, no. I would prefer something else.' 'All right, so let me ask myself, why did I have this bad feeling? What brought this about?' 'Well, I know that feelings come from beliefs.' 'So, what kind of a belief would I have to have about my relationship to this situation in order for me to have a bad feeling about it?'"

### The Full Chain

> "When you change it for a belief you would prefer, then that belief will generate a feeling that you prefer, which will also generate thoughts that you prefer, which will also generate behavior that you prefer."

```
THE BELIEF INVESTIGATION PROCESS

  Step 1: ACKNOWLEDGE the feeling
  "I feel bad. So what? I acknowledge it."
          ↓
  Step 2: ASK if you prefer it
  "Do I prefer this feeling? No."
          ↓
  Step 3: IDENTIFY the belief
  "What would I have to BELIEVE about
   my relationship to this situation
   to feel this way?"
          ↓
  Step 4: FIND OUT why you had it
  "Why did I adopt this belief?"
          ↓
  Step 5: LET IT GO
  "I can release this belief now"
          ↓
  Step 6: REPLACE with preferred belief
  New belief → New feeling →
  New thoughts → New behavior
```

## Experiences Reinforce Beliefs but Don't Generate Them

### The Cart Before the Horse

> "The way you've been taught to look at reality is making you think that the experience generates the belief. But in fact the experience first came from the belief."

> "Once you have the experience, the experience may reinforce the belief, but it doesn't generate the belief. The belief generates the experience."

### Why Beliefs Self-Reinforce

> "All beliefs must by definition contain an element, a component that is a self-generating, self-fulfilling aspect. If beliefs did not make it seem as if no other beliefs were possible, you wouldn't be able to have that particular experience."

### How to Use This

> "The experience is not causing the belief. It's just reinforcing the belief. And therefore the experience can be used to put you in touch with the belief that generated the experience to begin with."

```
THE BELIEF-EXPERIENCE RELATIONSHIP

  BELIEF (comes first)
      ↓ generates
  EXPERIENCE (comes second)
      ↓ reinforces
  BELIEF (seems even more "true")
      ↓ generates
  MORE EXPERIENCE (same pattern)
      ↓ reinforces further
  BELIEF (now seems like "the only truth")

  THE WAY OUT:
  Recognize the experience is
  REINFORCING the belief,
  not CAUSING it.
  
  Use the experience to FIND
  the belief that generated it.
  Change the belief →
  Change the experience.
```
