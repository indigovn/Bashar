## The Architecture Metaphor

### The Building Components

| Component | Represents |
|-----------|------------|
| **Blueprint** | Beliefs (the foundation of everything) |
| **Builders** | Emotions (energy in motion - reinforcers) |
| **Building Materials** | Thought patterns |
| **Structure** | Behaviors (what you are constructing) |

### Tracing Back to the Blueprint

> "Anytime you have a feeling, an emotion, a thought, thought pattern, a belief that leads to an action, physical action, you can always trace those actions, those behaviors, those thoughts, and those feelings back to what you have to believe to be true in order to feel, think, and behave the way you do."

### When the Structure Seems Off-Kilter

If the structure of your physical reality seems off-kilter:

1. **Trace it back** - like an architect to the original blueprint
2. **Find the misalignment** - beliefs that are out of alignment with who you are
3. **Understand the effect** - that misalignment shows up through builders, materials, and structure
4. **Correct at the source** - go back to the blueprint, back to the beliefs

### The Investigation Process

To correct misaligned beliefs:

1. Find out **why** you believe what you believe
2. Ask yourself questions with **honesty**
3. Have **willingness** to discover why you chose this belief
4. Understand it's part of your chosen challenge and lesson
5. Decide whether to **keep** or **replace** the belief
6. Replace with a belief more in alignment with who you are

### The Result of Alignment

When beliefs are aligned:
- The builders can work more cleanly
- The materials will be more solid
- The structure will be true
- Your physical reflection and experience will reflect that alignment
