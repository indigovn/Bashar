## The Triad — Intention, Action, Zero Expectation

### The Mechanism of Manifestation

> "The concept of intention as it must be aligned with action and married to lack of expectation."

> "This is the triad. The mechanism for remaining in balance, for remaining in your center, for remaining in the present, for living in the now, while at the same time making yourself literally most magnetically attractive."

### The Three Components

**1. INTENTION**

> "Will be most highly reflective of your idea of who and what ideal yourself to be."

Your intention reflects your highest vision of yourself. It is the starting point — the vision of who you truly are.

**2. ACTION**

> "You will take your intention and actualize it through physiological action to show through that action that you are willing to be open to the attraction. It grounds the intention. It grounds the idea, locks it in your physiological reality. Your actions are the underscoring of your intention."

> "You can intend something all day long. If you're not willing to show by your actions that you are aligned with that idea, it will remain in the realm of ether."

**3. ZERO EXPECTATION**

> "You must go forward in these actions with a full intention that what you imagine to be true for you is true. But you must have absolutely zero expectation that it has to manifest exactly as you originally thought."

```
THE TRIAD — MECHANISM OF MANIFESTATION

       INTENTION
      (who you truly are)
           ↓
       ACTION
      (grounds the intention
       in physical reality)
           ↓
    ZERO EXPECTATION
      (allows the infinite
       to surprise you)
           ↓
    ┌────────────────────────────────┐
    │  RESULT: You are in the NOW   │
    │  Magnetically attractive      │
    │  Open to the unexpected       │
    │  Receiving what's meant       │
    │  for you — or BETTER          │
    └────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Why Zero Expectation Is Essential

> "This balancing act is what allows you to remain open to the unexpected surprises and expansiveness of the infinite."

> "When you insist that the manifestation must occur exactly as you have imagined, you are actually short-changing yourself and not allowing the manifestation to come about in a way that might even be far more expanded than you were capable of imagining."

> "Allow yourself to have a healthy dose of the unexpected. Let the unknown be your friend."

| With Expectation | With Zero Expectation |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Short-changing yourself | Allowing the infinite to deliver |
| Limited to what you can imagine | Open to what's beyond your imagination |
| Structures of insistence block flow | Removing structures allows full flow |
| Result: what you imagined at best | Result: what you imagined **or better** |

### The Paradox

> "By having no expectation that it must come out that way, you will paradoxically be allowing it the best opportunity to come out perhaps not only the way you did originally imagine it in its best possible format, but you will be allowing it to come out perhaps even better than you imagined."
