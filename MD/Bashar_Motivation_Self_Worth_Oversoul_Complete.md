## The Mechanism of Motivation — Why You Choose What You Don't Prefer

### The Universal Law of Motivation

> "Always, without fail, without exception, automatically and immediately, no exceptions, you will always choose what you define, what you believe to be in your best interest. You will always move away from what you define, what you believe to be not in your best interests."

### The Hidden Mechanism

If you recognize something as your highest excitement but still don't choose it, there is **only one reason**:

> "You've attached a definition or a belief to that thing that makes it appear to not be the preferable choice over the other choice."

```
What You TRULY Prefer (from higher perspective)
              ↓
Negative belief ATTACHED to it
  ("If I choose this, something horrible will happen")
              ↓
Preferred thing now APPEARS more painful
  than the status quo
              ↓
You choose the status quo
  = "The lesser of two evils"
```

### The "Lesser of Two Evils" Principle

> "Even though what you're doing may be painful, you will always choose what you deem to be less painful than the other choice."

| What's Happening | How It Feels | The Truth |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Current situation causes pain | Familiar, bearable | You've chosen the "lesser evil" |
| Preferred choice seems worse | Terrifying, unpleasant | A false belief is making it seem worse |
| You stay stuck | Confusing — "Why do I keep choosing this?" | You ARE choosing your best interest — based on your current definitions |

### The Process to Break Free

```
Step 1: Recognize you're not choosing your preference
              ↓
Step 2: Ask: "What would I have to believe is true
         to make that thing seem NOT preferable?"
              ↓
Step 3: Be willing to hear the answer
              ↓
Step 4: Identify the belief
              ↓
Step 5: See that it's nonsensical and illogical
         — it doesn't belong to you
              ↓
Step 6: Let it go (now possible because you've
         identified it)
```

> "When you identify what that belief is, you will see that it's nonsensical and illogical because it doesn't belong to you. And thus then you can let it go. But you have to identify it first because you cannot work with what you don't know about."
