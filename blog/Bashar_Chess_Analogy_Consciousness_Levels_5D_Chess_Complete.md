# Bashar: The Chess Analogy — Consciousness Levels, Parallel Reality Strategy & 5D Chess - Complete Teaching

## Overview

This transmission is one of Bashar's most elaborate and structured teachings — a full extended analogy mapping the entire architecture of consciousness onto the game of chess. It begins by laying out the complete hierarchy from oversoul down to physical personality, with each level playing a specific role in constructing the whole person. The oversoul plays many games at once (each incarnation is a different game, some chess, some checkers, some cards). The individual soul focuses on one style of game. The higher mind is the strategist/player. The template level reality creates the game board. The collective agreement defines the general rules (gravity, time, sleep, food). The individual agreement defines the rules for your specific piece (how you move, where you fit). The physical personality is the actual piece itself. The teaching then deepens into parallel reality strategy (looking multiple moves ahead = perceiving parallel realities), warns against cluttering the board with too much information about other incarnations, reveals that even a pawn can win the game (holographic power of every piece), gives the powerful practice of actually playing physical chess as anchoring meditation, introduces 3D chess (current parallel realities) and 4D chess (time-factor chess — yours will eventually play this), and climaxes with the stunning revelation of **5th Dimensional Chess** — a game where ALL players win simultaneously, where you're not playing against opponents but aligning with them like a martial art, matching their movement so perfectly that your piece arrives in the winning position effortlessly.

## Don't Clutter the Board with Too Many Games

### The Warning

> "Many people on your planet become enamored, become fixated and focused on the idea of knowing about all these other parallel incarnations, parallel realities."

### Only What's Relevant

> "It's all well and good to know about them — but only to a certain point. Because once you allow yourself to understand how to download that information in the most efficient and relevant way, you will realize that you really only need a little bit of information here and a little bit of information there, just exactly when you need it to help you with the next move."

### The Cluttered Board Analogy

> "If you take it a little bit too far for its own sake... you will simply clutter up the game board to such an extent that you won't have any capability of making any moves at all."

### The Multiple Games Metaphor

> "It would be like playing a game of chess and somebody comes along and says, 'Well, here's another set of pieces. Let me put them on the board, too. And here's another set of pieces. Let me put them on the board, too.' Pretty soon, you'll not be able to make a move because all the squares will be filled."

### Mixed Game Types Make It Worse

> "Your oversoul and all these different incarnational games being played are not only just games of chess. Some of them are games of checkers, cards, and other types of games. So to ask for more information than you need would be like someone not only placing three sets of chess pieces on the board, but also dumping a lot of checkers on the board, dumping a bunch of cards on the board, and saying, 'Now go ahead and play.'"

```
OVER-CLUTTERING THE BOARD

  Your current game of chess
              ↓
  You ask: "Show me all parallel games!"
              ↓
  Extra chess pieces placed on board
  Plus checkers from another game
  Plus cards from another game
  Plus domino tiles...
              ↓
  BOARD NOW OBSCURED
  Cannot see where to move
  Cannot make any progress
              ↓
  "Keep it simple. Keep it streamlined."
  Only download what you NEED, WHEN you need it.
```
