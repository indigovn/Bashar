# Aluna: Transmute Anything Through Gratitude - Complete Teaching

## Overview

In this deeply personal transmission, Aluna — an akashic channeler and psycho-spiritual healer — shares a recent life-altering initiation experience in Peru. What began as a sacred ceremony with one of Peru's most revered altomisayoqs (lightning shamans) extended into an unexpected emotional shock and betrayal. Through this crucible, Aluna discovered what she calls "a secret of the universe": the power of gratitude to transmute any experience into liberation. She teaches that every struggle is God's gift, every betrayal can become a blessing, and the daily choice between praise and complaint determines the depth of our spiritual life.

## The Peru Initiation

### Background

Aluna led an advanced 12–18 day spiritual journey through Peru. This was not a publicly announced retreat — she intuitively selected and personally invited each participant based on their readiness for advanced work.

On the very final night of this journey, Aluna was invited to participate in a sacred initiation with an altomisayoq — one of the highest forms of Andean mystics and medicine people.

> "I was invited to be initiated in one of the oldest lineages of the alchemists... carrying her lineage — this woman named Maria."

---

## The Altomisayoq Tradition

**Altomisayoq** (Quechua: "chosen by lightning") — the most revered shamans, mystics, and medicine people in Peru.

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Selection** | Chosen by being struck by lightning (often multiple times) |
| **Abilities** | Commune with various beings and elementals |
| **Navigation** | Move through different worlds with ease |
| **Communication** | Hear the words on the wind, speak with mountains (Apus) |
| **Status** | Most revered spiritual practitioners in Peruvian tradition |

### What Happened

- The ceremony itself was profoundly mystical
- That same night, an emotionally shocking experience occurred with people on the journey
- The experience involved betrayal — choices made by others that deeply impacted Aluna
- When she expressed her shock and hurt, it was **not received well**
- She was left to process entirely on her own

> "I recognized this experience that I'm having right now is still part of the initiation. This emotional shock is actually a carrying over of the initiation that I just went through."

## Processing Alone: Grief, Hurt, and Shock

### The First Day

Aluna spent the entire day alone, sitting beside the **Apus** (sacred mountains of Peru), moving through layers of emotion:

**The Waves of Grief:**
- First layer: grief and sorrow
- Paired with shock at the hurtfulness of what happened
- Later waves included rage, despair, and helplessness

> "It is a process that comes in these beautiful waves of emotion. The rise and the fall. Sometimes accompanied with rage, sometimes accompanied only with despair, with helplessness."

### The Critical Choice

She recognized she had two paths:

| Path | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fall into the human drama** | "I cannot believe he did that. I cannot believe she thinks that's okay." — easy, seductive, victim-oriented |
| **Transmute through gratitude** | See the experience as God's gift — difficult, liberating, sovereign |

---

## The Shift: When Gratitude Breaks Through

### The Turning Point

Halfway through the first day of processing grief, while sitting beside the Apus and crying, something shifted:

> "There's just this light that goes on inside of me and I think, 'Wow, this experience is exactly what I needed somehow. This is exactly what I needed.'"

### What Followed

1. She began expressing gratitude to **every person** involved in the painful experience
2. The more she expressed gratitude, the more she was lifted into **absolute clarity**
3. She began tuning into the true ripple effects the experience would have on her life
4. She felt **liberation and wholeness** — greater than anything she had yet experienced

> "I began to tune into the feeling of liberation and wholeness. And I realized this experience was leading to my ultimate liberation and wholeness, greater than any liberation or wholeness I had yet experienced in my life."

### The Ceremony of Blessing

Moved by this energy of gratitude, Aluna entered into a personal ceremony to **bless each person** who had been involved in the painful experience — recognizing them not as betrayers but as instruments of divine purpose.

## The Temptation of the Human Story

### The Easy Path

> "So easy, so easy to fall prey and victim to the human story, the human drama."

Aluna identifies how tempting it is to:
- Blame others ("I cannot believe he did that")
- Judge their choices ("What were they thinking?")
- Seek validation from others about being wronged
- Abandon spiritual practice in moments of crisis

### The Daoist Teaching

> "The first hardest step is to begin something. And then the next hardest thing after that is to see it through to its completion."

This became her ongoing mantra: **"I am going to see this through to the end."**

### The Real Question

> "Are you going to see this through to the end?"

The initiation was not just the ceremony. It was the question: **Can you hold the truth of liberation and gratitude through weeks of grief, rage, and upheaval — or will you collapse into the human drama?**

## Transmuting Everything Through Gratitude

### A Secret of the Universe

> "You can transmute anything — anything — through gratitude. Anything. It is the path of liberation."

### Why Gratitude Works

> "Being so grateful because you are expressing your thanks to God — trusting, having full faith that God gave you that experience for your very highest. No matter what it looks like on the outside."

### Aluna's Daily Practice During Integration


### Not Spiritual Bypassing

Aluna emphasizes this is **not bypassing**:

- She let herself cry fully
- She felt everything that needed to be felt
- She let all of it move through her
- She cried **for herself** — not blaming anyone, not out of self-pity
- **Then** she turned to gratitude

> "I would let myself cry and cry and feel exactly what I needed to feel. I would let all of it go, move through me. And then I would say, 'And what about the gratitude?'"

### The Ho'oponopono Practice

Aluna used the **Ho'oponopono prayer** to bring her human self into alignment with the consciousness her spirit was inviting her into.

> "Did it take a few practices of the Ho'oponopono prayer to get my human on board and understanding and lifted to the same level of consciousness that my spirit was inviting me into, to actually feel the gratitude? Of course. But that is the practice."

## Your Struggles Are Your Greatest Teachers

### The Testing Pattern

> "We will all be tested again and again and again because it's our soul — it's spirit asking, 'Have you really mastered that?'"

**What Spirit Tests:**
- Compassion
- Forgiveness
- Gratitude
- Faith — trusting in the absolute that it is perfect somehow, no matter what it looks like

### The Invitation in Every Struggle

> "Your greatest struggle is your greatest teacher. It's you. It's God."

When struggling, ask:

1. **"What is here for me?"**
2. **"Why are you giving me this experience?"**
3. **"What do I have to be grateful for in this experience?"**
4. **"What do I have to learn from this experience?"**

### The Contemplation Requirement

> "You're not going to find an answer on ChatGPT."

Aluna sat for **six hours** in contemplation right after the shock happened. This work requires:
- Your own contemplation and critical thinking
- Talking to yourself aloud or recording voice memos
- Journaling
- Being alone with yourself and God

> "It's with you and God. That's it."

## Practical Framework: Moving Through Crisis

### Aluna's Process for Transmuting Difficult Experiences

```
Step 1: FEEL FULLY
   │  Let the grief, rage, despair come
   │  Cry for yourself (not blaming others)
   │  Do not resist or suppress
   ↓
Step 2: RECOGNIZE THE INITIATION
   │  Ask: "What is here for me?"
   │  See the experience as part of
   │  a larger spiritual purpose
   ↓
Step 3: SHIFT TO GRATITUDE
   │  Express gratitude for each person involved
   │  Thank God for the experience
   │  Trust it serves your highest good
   ↓
Step 4: PRACTICE HO'OPONOPONO
   │  Use prayer/practice to align your
   │  human self with your spirit's knowing
   ↓
Step 5: BLESS THOSE INVOLVED
   │  Enter ceremony or intention to bless
   │  every person connected to the experience
   ↓
Step 6: SEE IT THROUGH
   │  Repeat daily as waves of emotion return
   │  Keep showing up with sincerity
   │  Do not collapse into the human drama
   ↓
Step 7: RECEIVE THE LIBERATION
      Allow the wholeness and clarity to land
      "One day it just lands"
```

### Key Distinctions

| This Practice IS | This Practice IS NOT |
|---|---|
| Feeling fully, then choosing gratitude | Skipping over emotions |
| Seeing God in the experience | Denying the pain happened |
| Blessing those who hurt you | Excusing harmful behavior |
| Contemplating alone with God | Seeking external answers |
| A daily repeated practice | A one-time realization |
| Showing up sincerely | Performing spirituality |

## Key Principles Summary

- Gratitude is "a secret of the universe" — it can transmute **anything**
- Gratitude is the path of liberation
- Expressing gratitude = expressing thanks to God = trusting God fully
- The more you praise, the more truth reveals itself

- Do not "play at spirituality" — live it

- The hardest part is not beginning — it is seeing it through to the end
- However much you show up, God matches and multiplies it

### On Daily Life
- Every day is a choice between praise and complaint
- Both are always available; choose praise
- Your greatest struggle is your greatest teacher
- Sit with your own contemplation — the answers are between you and God
