# The Three Guides (via Aluna): The Kybalion — The Law of Mind - Complete Teaching

## Overview

This is the first transmission in the "Mysteries of the Kybalion" series, channeled through Aluna from her three spirit guides. It explores the Kybalion's foundational principle — **"All is Mental"** — from a 21st-century perspective. The guides examine the nature of the untrained mind (mischievous, dust-kicking, chaos-seeking), contrast it with the playful heart, and offer a concrete daily practice for retraining mental awareness. The core teaching: your mind shapes your reality, and through willpower, heart-awareness, and disciplined practice, you can clear the mental dust and begin to see — and create — from clarity.

## The First Principle: All Is Mental

> "All is mental. This is the law. This is the first principle that one must understand, know, and become — in order to see how the universe unfolds before him exactly as he imagines."

### The Three Levels of Engagement

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Understand** | Intellectual grasp of the principle |
| **Know** | Experiential knowing through practice |
| **Become** | Embodied living of the principle |

The law states: reality is shaped by mind. Your perspectives, beliefs, opinions, and "jagged edges" in the mind are what construct the world you perceive and experience.

### The Two Responses to This Law

> "Some choose to soften these edges. Others choose to become even more sharper still. There is benefit in both."

> "One must find balance between these two extremes, these two poles — but that is a lesson for a later time."

## What Does Your Mind Look Like?

The guides open with a direct diagnostic question — an invitation to honestly assess the current state of your mental landscape.

> "What does your mind look like? Is it a mess that needs to be cleaned up? Is there always a sweeping, circling energy buzzing through your head, causing distortion? Rising dust up, bringing ashes from your past to swirl around and cloud your vision?"

### The Dust Metaphor

The untrained mind is characterized by **dust** — residue from past experiences, wounds, and unresolved emotions that circulates continuously, clouding present perception.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────┐
│         THE DUSTY MIND           │
│                                  │
│    Past wounds ──→ ☁ Dust ☁      │
│    Old beliefs ──→ ☁ swirls ☁    │
│    Opinions    ──→ ☁ around ☁    │
│                                  │
│    Result: Clouded vision        │
│    Reality shaped by the past    │
└──────────────────────────────────┘
```

> "This must be cleared. The dust must be allowed to settle — if you are to shape a reality that is not based on past experiences and wounding."

## The Mischievous Mind vs. The Playful Heart

### Two Distinct Qualities

The guides make a precise distinction between the energy of mind and the energy of heart:

| Quality | The Mind | The Heart |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Mischievous | Playful |
| **Movement** | Like a cat — coming, going, jumping in and out | Light, easy, gentle |
| **Effect** | Kicks up dust, creates distortion | Moves energy through without dust |
| **Relationship to you** | Toys with you | Guides you |

> "Settling the mind is tricky business, especially on the Earth plane. How the mind likes to play with you, toy with you — like a cat. Coming, going, jumping in and out. Mischievous is the mind."

### The Balancing Principle

> "The mind can be balanced with the heart. The heart is playful. The mind is mischievous. There is a difference between these two qualities."

> "Learning to play with the heart brings balance to the mind. It gives the mind a new way to play — to remove and move energies through it."

**Key insight:** The goal is not to kill the mind's activity but to give it a **new way to play** — one that moves energy through without kicking up dust.

```
  MIND ALONE               MIND + HEART
  ──────────               ────────────
  Energy moves     →    Energy moves
  Dust kicks up    →    Energy flows through
  Distortion       →    Clarity
  Mischief         →    Play
  Chaos            →    Balance
```

## The Retraining: Conscious Seeing

### The Counterintuitive Method

The guides offer a surprising technique: instead of fighting the mind's tendency to find what's wrong, **use it deliberately**.

> "The mind has a tendency, a habit, and even a like and enjoyment of seeing what is wrong everywhere. So do so."

### The Process

```
Step 1: NOTICE ALL THE CHAOS
   │  See the dust in the corners
   │  See all the cracks that surround you
   │  Let the mind do what it loves to do
   ↓
Step 2: EXHAUST THE MIND
   │  Bring conscious attention to every flaw
   │  Use YOUR awareness to direct this
   │  (not the mind's unconscious scanning)
   ↓
Step 3: LET IT SIT AND BE QUIET
   │  "When you have exhausted all things
   │  in your space, in your environment,
   │  it will not have much else to talk about"
   ↓
Step 4: MOVE INTO A NEW SPACE
   │  Begin to see what you are grateful for
   │  Notice what brings you joy
   │  See the spaces of light
   │  Notice the people who lift you up
   │  Notice what makes you smile
   ↓
   RESULT: A retrained mind
```

**Why this works:** By consciously directing the mind's fault-finding rather than letting it run unconsciously, you bring it under your awareness. Once it exhausts its material, a natural silence opens — and you can redirect toward gratitude and joy.

> "Bring your attention to these spaces so that your mind can stop bringing attention to it at times that are inconvenient or that are unimportant."

## Willpower: The Engine of Transformation

### The Nature of Willpower

> "Willpower comes from deep within your being, and you channel it up through your mental energy in order to accomplish the things you set out to do."

### The Promise

> "If you put your full attention, energy, and awareness on transforming your mind — you will succeed. You will. Your will will help you succeed. Remember this."

### The Honest Warning

The mind will not simply sit and obey for long:

> "Once it learns your tricks, it will jump up again, trying to get your attention. And yes, this is its job. And yes, this is what you signed up for when you chose to enter this human body and experience on this Earth plane."

### The Right Response

> "Do not condemn yourself. Do not judge yourself. That will simply pull you farther away from where you wish to be."

> "Instead, learn to play with your mind as the heart would. Learn to be light, easy, and gentle when it comes to your mind pulling you away — but firm enough to redirect your attention elsewhere when the time is necessary and appropriate."

**The balance:** Light and gentle **AND** firm enough to redirect. Not harsh discipline. Not passive surrender. Playful firmness.

## Where You Place Attention, That Grows

### The Universal Principle

> "The attention should always be placed on that which you want more of. This is common knowledge that you all know. Your attention — where you place it — that is what grows larger, bigger, stronger, and even faster."

### The Trap

> "So do you not agree it would be a waste of time then to place your attention on your mind and how it creates distortions? How the shadows grow in there? The spinning cycle that many of you are familiar with — going around and around and around, getting nowhere fast, and certainly nowhere helpful."

### The Redirection

Instead of focusing on what's wrong with your mind, focus on what you want more of:

| Don't Place Attention On | Do Place Attention On |
|---|---|
| Mental distortions | Clarity |
| Spinning thought-cycles | Calm |
| Shadows in the mind | The light of the heart |
| What's wrong | What you're grateful for |
| Emotional reactivity | Detached, clear mindset |

### The Dust-on-the-Heart Problem

### The Heart Drop

The practice involves **dropping awareness** from the mental space down into the heart center:

> "Turning your mental awareness there and dropping into your heart space — so that you can feel how and where to go toward for this to be accomplished."

What you want more of:
- **Clarity**
- **Calm**
- **A detached mindset** — not allowing emotions to guide you
- **Heart-centered perception**

## The Pandora's Box Visualization

The guides offer a specific visualization technique for containing the scattered mind:

> "There are an infinite number of ways to bring the mind into a balanced state — to gather all of the winds that have been scattered, like Pandora's Box. Bring them inside. Collect them."

### The Technique

1. **Visualize a sacred container** — a box, a holder, something within your own mind
2. **Gather the scattered winds** (thoughts, worries, reactions) and bring them inside
3. **Shut the lid** — do not let them open unless you command it
4. **Experience the clarity** that comes, even if only for a moment

> "Do not let them open unless you command it to be so."

### The Growth Principle

> "Remember that your willpower will grow. Therefore, your ability to keep this box closed will grow — because it is with your own mental fortitude that it stays shut."

The duration of clarity will increase with practice — the lid stays shut longer as willpower strengthens.

## Key Principles Summary

### On the Nature of Mind
- All is mental — this is the first and foundational Hermetic principle
- The mind shapes reality through its perspectives, beliefs, opinions, and "jagged edges"
- The untrained mind is mischievous — like a cat, toying with you, kicking up dust
- The dust of the mind is residue from past experiences and wounds that clouds present vision

### On Mind and Heart
- The mind is **mischievous**; the heart is **playful** — a crucial distinction
- The heart balances the mind by giving it a new way to play — one that doesn't kick up dust
- The heart is your ever-guide; its light shines even in the darkest nights
- Mental dust can settle over the heart, making its light feel distant

### On Willpower and Attention
- Willpower comes from deep within and channels upward through mental energy
- Full attention, energy, and awareness on transforming the mind **will** succeed
- Where you place attention, that grows — so place it on what you want more of
- Do not focus on the mind's distortions; focus on clarity, calm, and heart-light

### On Practice
- Understanding the laws intellectually is not enough — they must be practiced
- The mind will learn your tricks and jump up again; this is its job
- Do not condemn or judge yourself — that pulls you farther away
- Be light and gentle **and** firm enough to redirect — playful firmness
- Willpower grows with practice; the ability to maintain clarity increases over time

### On the Daily Practice
- Set a daily intention: clear mind, open heart
- When the mind takes over, collect the scattered thoughts into a sacred container
- Drop awareness from the head into the center of the chest
- Open the heart with breath and awareness
- Repeat as many times as needed, resetting intention each time
